Laboratory Safety Guidelines

Importance of Laboratory Safety

Laboratory safety encompasses all practices, protocols, and behaviors designed to prevent accidents, injuries, and exposure to hazardous materials. It's essential for protecting individuals, research integrity, and the environment.

Common Laboratory Hazards

Chemical Hazards

  • Toxic substances causing acute or chronic health issues.
  • Flammable/explosive materials.
  • Corrosive chemicals like strong acids and bases.
  • Reactive substances that may ignite or explode.

Biological Hazards

  • Pathogenic microorganisms (bacteria, viruses).
  • Allergens and biological toxins.

Physical Hazards

  • Sharp instruments, broken glass.
  • Noise and radiation.

Ergonomic Hazards

  • Repetitive strain injuries and poor posture.

Psychological Hazards

  • Stress and fatigue from long hours or high workloads.

Essential Lab Safety Rules

  1. Wear appropriate PPE: lab coat, gloves, goggles.
  2. Know where safety equipment is located and how to use it.
  3. Never work alone in the lab.
  4. No food or drink allowed.
  5. Label all chemicals correctly.
  6. Understand chemical properties before use.
  7. Conduct only authorized experiments.
  8. Dispose of waste properly.
  9. Keep workspace clean and organized.
  10. Never pipette by mouth.
  11. Use fume hoods for volatile substances.
  12. Wear closed-toe shoes and cover skin.
  13. Report accidents immediately.
  14. Know emergency procedures.
  15. Handle glassware with care.
  16. Check electrical equipment regularly.
  17. Use caution with gas cylinders and vacuum systems.
  18. No children or pets allowed in labs.
  19. Assess risks and prepare for emergencies.
  20. Promote a culture of safety at all times.
